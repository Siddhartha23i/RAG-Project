SECTION 2: CHRONOLOGICAL TIMELINE (1996-2025)
================================================================================
[1996] "BackRub" search engine developed by Page and Brin at Stanford.
[1997] Domain "google.com" registered on September 15.
[1998] Company incorporated in Susan Wojcicki’s garage. $100k investment from Andy Bechtolsheim.
[1999] Moved to Palo Alto. $25M funding from Sequoia and Kleiner Perkins.
[2000] AdWords launched (Self-service advertising model). Became default Yahoo! search provider.
[2001] Eric Schmidt hired as CEO ("Adult Supervision"). Google Images launched.
[2002] Google News launched. Yahoo! attempts acquisition for $3B (rejected).
[2003] AdSense launched (contextual ads for publishers). Moved to Googleplex.
[2004] IPO (Initial Public Offering) at $85/share. Gmail launched (beta) with 1GB storage. Orkut launched.
[2005] Google Maps, Google Earth, Google Talk launched. Android Inc. acquired ($50M).
[2006] YouTube acquired ($1.65B). Google Docs/Spreadsheets (now Sheets) launched.
[2007] DoubleClick acquired ($3.1B). Street View launched. Android announced (Open Handset Alliance).
[2008] Google Chrome browser launched. T-Mobile G1 (first Android phone) released.
[2009] AdMob acquired ($750M). Google Voice launched.
[2010] Google Buzz (failed). Nexus One phone released.
[2011] Larry Page returns as CEO. Google+ launched (social network). Motorola Mobility acquired ($12.5B).
[2012] Google Drive launched. Knowledge Graph introduced. Google Glass demoed.
[2013] Waze acquired ($966M). Chromecast released. Google Reader shutdown (infamous).
[2014] DeepMind acquired ($500M+). Nest Labs acquired ($3.2B). Motorola sold to Lenovo ($2.9B).
[2015] CORPORATE RESTRUCTURE: Alphabet Inc. formed. Sundar Pichai becomes Google CEO.
[2016] "AI First" strategy announced. Google Assistant, Google Home, Pixel phone launched. Waymo spun out.
[2017] AlphaGo defeats Ke Jie. HTC mobile division talent acquired ($1.1B).
[2018] "Don't be evil" removed from preface of code of conduct. Duplex AI demo.
[2019] Page and Brin step down from daily roles; Pichai takes over Alphabet. Stadia launched.
[2020] Google Workspace rebrand (formerly G Suite). Antitrust lawsuits from DOJ.
[2021] Fitbit acquisition completed ($2.1B). LaMDA (Language Model) introduced.
[2022] Mandiant acquired ($5.4B). Code Red declared on AI due to ChatGPT release.
[2023] Bard (chatbot) launched. Gemini model announced. DeepMind and Google Brain merge.
[2024] Gemini rebrand (Bard becomes Gemini). Major layoffs (~12,000 employees). 
[2025] Focus on AGI (Artificial General Intelligence), Quantum Computing milestones, and regulatory battles (Ad tech monopoly trials).

SECTION 3: TECHNICAL INFRASTRUCTURE (THE "GOOGLE STACK")
================================================================================
DATA_CENTERS:
- Global network of hyperscale data centers (The Dalles, Council Bluffs, Hamina, St. Ghislain, etc.).
- Custom Hardware: TPUs (Tensor Processing Units) for AI workloads, Argos VCU for YouTube.

SOFTWARE_INFRASTRUCTURE:
1. GFS (Google File System) / Colossus: Distributed file system replacement for GFS.
2. MapReduce: Historical framework for processing large data sets (replaced largely by Flume/Cloud Dataflow).
3. BigTable: Distributed storage system for structured data.
4. Spanner: Globally distributed, synchronously replicated database (TrueTime API using atomic clocks).
5. Borg: Cluster manager (precursor to Kubernetes) for scheduling jobs.
6. Chubby: Lock service for loosely coupled distributed systems.
7. Protocol Buffers (Protobuf): Language-neutral serialization format.
8. GRPC: High-performance RPC framework.

AI_FRAMEWORKS:
- TensorFlow: Open-source machine learning library.
- JAX: High-performance numerical computing (autodiff).
- Keras: High-level neural networks API (integrated).

SECTION 7: ANTITRUST & CONTROVERSIES
================================================================================
EUROPEAN UNION:
- Shopping Case (2017): €2.42B fine (Favoring own comparison shopping).
- Android Case (2018): €4.34B fine (Illegal restrictions on Android device makers).
- AdSense Case (2019): €1.49B fine (Abusive practices in online ads).

UNITED STATES:
- US v. Google (2020/2023): DOJ lawsuit alleging monopoly in Search and Search Advertising.
- US v. Google (2023): DOJ lawsuit alleging monopoly in Ad Technology stack.
- Epic Games v. Google: Jury found Google Play Store an illegal monopoly (2023).

PRIVACY:
- Project Dragonfly: Censored search engine for China (Cancelled after employee protest).
- Project Maven: AI for DoD drone footage (Contract expired after employee protest).
- GDPR fines in EU.
